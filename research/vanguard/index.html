Advice Moments

Most of a self-directed investor's assets sit one step away from advice that could help them, and that step is almost never offered at the moment it would matter. Advice Moments is a governed personalization layer that surfaces the right advice option, to the right investor, at the right time, built as a phased and eval-governed AI system rather than a black box.

The problem, in one paragraph

Advice is a destination. It should be a moment.

Vanguard is one of the world's two largest asset managers, structured to be owned by its funds and, through them, its investors. That structure makes broadening access to advice both a mission and a growth lever, and leadership has publicly framed it as a strategic priority. Yet for a self-directed investor, advice is still mostly a place you have to decide to go to: a separate tab, a separate decision, a separate sales motion.

The result is an activation gap. The investor who would benefit most, the one navigating a rollover, a windfall, a new goal, or simply more complexity than a single index fund, rarely gets a relevant, plain-language prompt at the moment the need is live. The capability to detect that moment and respond to it well, in-product and at scale, is the opportunity.

Working backwards · the moment, from the investor's side

A long-time self-directed investor logs in after changing jobs. Instead of a generic dashboard, she sees one quiet, relevant prompt: given her rollover and rising cash balance, a short, plain explanation of how Vanguard's advice options could fit, the fee, the suitability, and a one-tap way to talk to someone or start digitally. She is not sold to. She is oriented. She books a consultation in under two minutes.

Where the value is

The advice continuum, and the gap that matters

Picture the continuum as a target, not a staircase. The self-directed majority forms the wide outer ring; clients thin out and assets concentrate toward the centre, where a small number of wealth relationships sit above $5M. Minimums and fees rise inward. Hover or tap any ring for the public thresholds.

Advice Moments works at the outer edge. The activation gap between the self-directed majority and the first human-advice step (Personal Advisor, from $50,000) is where the most movable assets sit and where the next best step is least often surfaced. Since advice fees run roughly 5 to 10 times fund fees, matching the right investor to the right step is both a service and a margin lever, when it genuinely fits.

The approach

A signal becomes a governed, well-timed nudge

Advice Moments is a closed loop. It listens for moments that suggest a real need, scores whether advice would genuinely benefit this investor, delivers a suitability-checked and explainable prompt, and routes to the right next step, human or digital. Every outcome feeds evals that tune the system.

01 · SIGNAL

Detect the moment

Life events, cash build-up, goal changes, complexity signals. Privacy-respecting and opt-out aware.

→
02 · SCORE

Eligibility & benefit

Would advice actually help here, and which tier fits? Rules first, then a propensity-and-benefit model.

→
03 · NUDGE

Governed prompt

Plain-language, suitability-gated, explainable, frequency-capped. Shows the fee and the fit honestly.

→
04 · ROUTE

Consult or enroll

One tap to a human consult or a digital start. Clean handoff with context to the advisor or flow.

↻ Outcomes (booked, enrolled, dismissed, complaint) feed evals that re-tune scoring, copy, and frequency, the system gets more useful and more careful over time.

How it ships

Three phases, each shippable and each de-risking the next

Start with the version that carries no model risk and is in front of investors fastest. Earn the right to add intelligence with evidence, not ambition.

Phase 0~1 quarter

Rules & library

  • Deterministic triggers (rollover detected, cash above threshold, goal added)
  • A curated library of plain-language advice prompts, suitability-gated
  • One surface, one or two moments, strict frequency caps
No ML risk. Proves the surface, the routing, and investor trust before any model exists.
Phase 1+1–2 quarters

Predictive scoring

  • Propensity-and-benefit model ranks who, which tier, and when
  • Holdout groups measure true incremental lift, not just clicks
  • Suppression model to prevent over-contacting
Adds precision on top of a surface investors already trust. Evals gate the release.
Phase 2later

Generative guidance

  • An LLM explains the recommendation and answers follow-ups in plain language
  • Strictly retrieval-grounded, no performance promises, no specific security advice
  • Human-in-the-loop for anything approaching a recommendation boundary
Highest value and highest risk, so it ships last, behind the strongest guardrails.

The part most specs skip

The AI is a behavioral contract, not a feature

A recommendation engine is probabilistic: the same investor can be scored differently as data shifts, and quality lives in distributions, not pass or fail. So the spec defines what good looks like in numbers, how the system fails safely, and where it must stop. In a fiduciary context this is not optional polish, it is the product.

Failure modes get as much attention as the happy path

Over-nudging erodes trust

Detect
Frequency caps, dismissal and complaint rates by cohort
Respond
Suppress, cool-down window, easy "show me less"
Alert
Cohort dismissal or opt-out spike trips an alarm
Recover
Auto-throttle; review copy and targeting before resuming

An unsuitable recommendation

Detect
Pre-send suitability gate; post-hoc audit sample
Respond
Hard block at the gate; nothing unsuitable ships
Alert
Any gate bypass pages the owning team immediately
Recover
Roll back rule or model version; document and re-eval

Model drift over time

Detect
Live score-distribution and lift monitoring vs. baseline
Respond
Fall back to Phase 0 rules if metrics breach
Alert
Drift threshold breach notifies on-call
Recover
Retrain, re-eval, staged re-release

Generative hallucination (Phase 2)

Detect
Grounded-generation evals; unsupported-claim classifier
Respond
Constrain to retrieved facts; refuse outside scope
Alert
Unsupported-claim rate over bar blocks deploy
Recover
Tighten retrieval and prompts; human review queue

Guardrails & governance

Reg BI / fiduciary suitability gate Human-in-the-loop at recommendation boundaries Frequency caps & cool-downs "Why am I seeing this?" explainability One-tap opt-out, honored everywhere No performance promises, no specific security advice Risk-classified & documented (EU AI Act-style) Bias & fairness review across cohorts

Governance is designed in, not bolted on: every prompt is suitable, explainable, capped, and reversible, and the whole system is documented to stand up to a regulator and to the firm's own fiduciary standard.

How we would know it works

One north star, an honest funnel, and guardrail metrics

North star
Appropriately advised assets

Assets moved to a genuinely suitable advice step, measured as true incremental lift against a holdout, never just clicks.

Activation funnel
Nudge → consult → enroll

Relevant prompt shown, consultation booked or digital flow started, then enrolled. Watch drop-off at each step.

Guardrail (must not regress)
Suitability · complaints · opt-outs

Suitability precision, complaint rate, and opt-out rate are release gates. Growth that moves these the wrong way is not growth.

Business outcome
Retention & advised mix

Advised relationships retain and deepen. Track downstream retention and the shift in advised vs. self-directed mix.
