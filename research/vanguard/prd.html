Advice Moments

A governed personalization layer that surfaces the right advice step, to the right self-directed investor, at the right moment.

Scope & discretion. Independent thinking exercise built entirely from public information. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or based on confidential information from Vanguard. Product names are public; all figures, thresholds, and timelines are illustrative and directional, intended to frame a conversation.

1 · Problem

For a self-directed investor, advice is still a destination, a separate decision and motion, when it should be a moment. The investor who would benefit most, navigating a rollover, a windfall, a new goal, or simply rising complexity, rarely gets a relevant, plain-language prompt while the need is live. The capability to detect that moment and respond to it well, in-product, at scale, and within fiduciary guardrails, is the opportunity.

Vanguard's investor-owned structure makes broadening access to advice both mission-aligned and a growth lever, and leadership has publicly framed it as a priority. The largest pool of movable assets and unmet need sits at the first transition of the advice continuum: self-directed investors never matched to the next appropriate step.

2 · Goals & non-goals

Goals
  • Grow appropriately advised assets via well-timed, relevant prompts
  • Match each investor to the genuinely suitable step (including staying self-directed)
  • Ship value fast with no model risk, then add intelligence behind evals
  • Make every prompt suitable, explainable, capped, and reversible
Non-goals
  • Maximizing clicks or up-tier conversions for their own sake
  • Specific security recommendations or performance promises
  • Replacing advisors; this routes to them with context
  • A persistent "get advice" ad rather than a moment-triggered prompt

4 · Solution overview

A closed loop: Signal (detect a real moment) → Score (would advice benefit, which tier) → Nudge (governed, explainable prompt) → Route (human consult or digital flow). Outcomes feed evals that re-tune scoring, copy, and frequency. The system should become both more useful and more careful over time.

5 · Functional requirements (phased)

Phase 0 · Rules & library (~1 quarter, no ML)

  • Deterministic triggers from public, first-party signals (e.g., rollover detected, cash above a threshold, a goal added)
  • Curated library of plain-language prompts, each pre-mapped to a suitable advice tier and pre-cleared by compliance
  • One surface, one or two moments, strict frequency caps and cool-downs
  • Explainability ("why am I seeing this") and one-tap opt-out from day one

Phase 1 · Predictive scoring (+1–2 quarters)

  • Propensity-and-benefit model ranks who, which tier, and when
  • Holdout groups measure true incremental lift, not clicks
  • Suppression model prevents over-contacting; release gated by evals

Phase 2 · Generative guidance (later)

  • LLM explains the recommendation and answers follow-ups in plain language
  • Strictly retrieval-grounded; no performance promises; no specific security advice
  • Human-in-the-loop for anything approaching a recommendation boundary

6 · The behavioral contract

The recommendation is probabilistic: the same investor can be scored differently as data shifts, and quality lives in distributions, not pass/fail. So the spec defines what good looks like numerically, how the system fails safely, and where it must stop.

6.1 Eval-driven acceptance criteria (illustrative)

DimensionHow measuredRelease bar
Suitability precisionAudited sample + rules check on every prompt≥ 99.5%; zero tolerance for clearly unsuitable
Incremental benefitHoldout vs. nudged; appropriate take-up liftPositive, significant over control
HelpfulnessHuman-rated relevance and clarity≥ 4.3 / 5 median
Non-coercion / toneLLM-as-judge + human review for pressure/hype0 coercive/misleading in sample
Factuality (Phase 2)Grounded-generation eval; unsupported-claim rate< 0.5%, block on fail

Ship in reporting mode first; compare automated scores to human judgment; then block deployment on trusted metrics for safety, suitability, and regression cases.

6.2 Failure modes

6.3 Guardrails & governance

Reg BI / fiduciary suitability gate Human-in-the-loop at recommendation boundaries Frequency caps & cool-downs Explainability ("why am I seeing this") One-tap opt-out, honored everywhere No performance promises / no security advice EU AI Act-style risk classification & documentation Bias & fairness review across cohorts

6.4 Data & model dependencies

  • First-party, consented signals only; clear lawful basis and retention limits
  • Suitability reference data and rules owned with compliance
  • Model registry, versioning, and rollback; eval suite in CI before any release
  • Phase 2: retrieval corpus restricted to approved, current, factual content

7 · Success metrics

  • North star: appropriately advised assets (incremental vs. holdout)
  • Funnel: relevant nudge → consult booked / digital start → enrolled
  • Guardrails (must not regress): suitability precision, complaint rate, opt-out rate
  • Business: downstream retention and advised vs. self-directed mix

8 · Go-to-market & rollout

  • Start on the highest-intent logged-in surface, one or two moments, small holdout-controlled cohort
  • Coordinate with advice operations so consult demand matches capacity
  • Expand surfaces and moments only as evals and guardrail metrics hold

9 · Risks & mitigations

RiskMitigation
Trust erosion from feeling "sold to"Moment-triggered relevance, honesty about fee/fit, hard caps, easy opt-out
Compliance throughput bottleneckPre-cleared library in Phase 0; templated, auditable prompts
Advisor capacity can't absorb demandThrottle by capacity; favor digital routes where suitable
Optimizing the wrong metricNorth star is incremental suitable assets, not clicks; guardrails gate release
